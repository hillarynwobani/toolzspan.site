How to Convert Images Into a PDF File

By Toolzspan Editorial Team · April 6, 2026

There are many reasons you might want to convert images into a PDF. Perhaps you photographed a multi-page document with your phone and need to combine the photos into one clean file. Maybe you are assembling a portfolio of your design work. Or you might need to submit scanned receipts as a single PDF for expense reporting.

Whatever the case, converting images to PDF is one of the most practical document skills you can learn. It turns scattered image files into a single, organized document that anyone can open, share, and print.

Why Convert Images to PDF?

PDF is the universal document format. Almost every device and operating system can open a PDF, and the layout stays consistent no matter where it is viewed. Converting your images to PDF gives you several advantages:

  • Professional appearance. A PDF looks more polished than a collection of loose image files. Sending five JPGs feels disorganized; sending one PDF feels deliberate.
  • Easy sharing. One file is easier to email or upload than multiple images. Many portals and forms only accept a single file upload.
  • Consistent viewing. PDFs maintain their layout on any screen, unlike images that may be reordered or resized by different apps.
  • Archive quality. PDFs are widely accepted for long-term document storage and are the standard format for government and legal archives.
  • Page ordering. A PDF preserves the exact order of your pages, while image files can get rearranged when copied between devices or folders.

What Image Formats Are Supported?

Most image-to-PDF converters accept the three most common image formats:

  • JPG (JPEG). The most widely used photo format. Excellent for photographs and scans. Uses lossy compression, so file sizes are small but some detail is lost.
  • PNG. Best for screenshots, diagrams, and images with text. Uses lossless compression, so every pixel is preserved perfectly. Larger file sizes than JPG.
  • WebP. A modern format developed by Google that combines good quality with small file sizes. Increasingly common on the web.

You can mix formats in a single conversion — upload some JPGs and some PNGs, and they will all be combined into one PDF without issues.

How to Convert Images to PDF

You can convert images to PDF for free using this Image to PDF converter. Here is how:

  1. Open the Image to PDF tool in your browser
  2. Upload your images — JPG, PNG, or WebP formats are supported
  3. Arrange the images in the order you want them to appear as pages
  4. Click convert to create your PDF
  5. Download the finished PDF document

Each image becomes a full page in the PDF. The page dimensions are automatically matched to each image, so nothing gets cropped or distorted.

Tips for Better Results

Prepare your images first

Before converting, make sure your images are clean and properly oriented. Rotate any sideways photos, crop out unnecessary borders, and check that the content is legible.

Compress images if the file is too large

If your images are high-resolution photos, the resulting PDF can be quite large. Consider compressing your images first using the Image Compressor to bring down the file size before conversion.

Use consistent image sizes

If possible, use images of similar dimensions. While the converter handles mixed sizes gracefully, a PDF with uniform page sizes looks more professional.

Common Use Cases

Digitizing Paper Documents

If you do not have a scanner, photographing documents with your phone and converting the photos to PDF is the next best thing. The result is a shareable, archivable document file.

Creating Photo Portfolios

Photographers and designers often need to present their work as a single document. Converting a selection of images to PDF creates a portable portfolio that can be emailed or printed.

Expense Reports

Many companies require receipts in PDF format. If you photographed your receipts, converting them to PDF satisfies this requirement without needing a scanner.

Submitting Assignments

Students who handwrite assignments can photograph each page and convert the images to a single PDF for submission through online portals.

After Conversion

Once your images are in PDF format, you might want to:

  • Compress the PDF to reduce file size
  • Add a watermark for branding or confidentiality
  • Run OCR to make the text in your images searchable
  • Merge it with other PDFs to create a complete document package
