How to Rotate Pages in a PDF File

By Toolzspan Editorial Team · April 17, 2026

Few things are more annoying than opening a PDF and finding that some pages are sideways or upside down. This happens more often than you might expect — scanners sometimes orient pages incorrectly, documents get rotated during email transmission, or a landscape page gets saved in portrait mode. Whatever the cause, fixing it is quick and easy.

Why Do PDF Pages Get Rotated?

There are several common reasons why pages in a PDF end up in the wrong orientation:

  • Scanner orientation. When feeding documents through a scanner, some pages may be placed upside down or sideways. The scanner captures them exactly as they are positioned, resulting in rotated pages in the output PDF.
  • Mixed document orientations. A report might contain mostly portrait pages but include a few landscape tables or charts. If the landscape pages are not properly flagged, they may display incorrectly.
  • Phone camera scans. When photographing documents with a phone, the camera's orientation sensor sometimes gets it wrong, especially if the phone is held at an angle.
  • File conversion artifacts. Converting between different document formats can occasionally introduce rotation errors, particularly when the source and target formats handle page orientation differently.
  • PDF creation software bugs. Some PDF generation tools have known issues with page orientation, especially when combining pages from multiple sources.

Understanding Rotation Degrees

Page rotation is measured in degrees, and understanding what each value does helps you make the right choice:

  • 90 degrees clockwise. Turns the page a quarter turn to the right. Use this when a portrait page needs to become landscape, or when content is rotated 90 degrees counterclockwise.
  • 180 degrees. Flips the page completely upside down. Use this when a page is inverted — text running from bottom to top.
  • 270 degrees clockwise (or 90 degrees counterclockwise). Turns the page a quarter turn to the left. Use this when content is rotated 90 degrees clockwise.

If you are not sure which rotation to apply, start with 90 degrees. If the result is not right, try 270 degrees (which is the opposite direction). If the page is upside down, use 180 degrees.

Rotating All Pages vs. Specific Pages

Sometimes every page in the PDF needs to be rotated — for example, if a scanner produced the entire document in the wrong orientation. In this case, you can specify "all" pages. Other times, only a few pages need correction — perhaps a scanned stack had one or two pages fed in the wrong direction. In this case, specify the individual page numbers that need fixing.

Most rotation tools accept page numbers in a comma-separated format (for example, "3,5,8") or as a range (for example, "3-8"). This gives you precise control over which pages are affected.

Common Scenarios

Fixing scanned documents

When you scan a multi-page document, it is easy for one or two pages to end up sideways — especially if the pages were not aligned perfectly in the scanner feed tray. Rotation fixes these pages without requiring you to rescan the entire document.

Correcting phone scans

Photos taken with a phone camera are particularly prone to orientation issues. If you converted phone photos to PDF using an Image to PDF tool and some pages are sideways, the page rotator fixes them in seconds.

Preparing documents for printing

Before printing a PDF, check that all pages are in the correct orientation. Printing a rotated page wastes paper and looks unprofessional. A quick rotation check saves time and materials.

After Rotating

Once your pages are properly oriented, you might want to:

  • Compress the PDF to reduce file size
  • Remove any blank pages that are not needed
  • Merge it with other documents into a complete package

Final Thoughts
